Chrysanthemum

The chrysanthemum, called "Kiku" in Japanese, symbolizes the autumn season, but more than that, it's a symbol of the country itself. The monarchy is referred to as the Chrysanthemum Throne and the imperial crest is a stylized mum blossom.
The flower is also a common motif in art, and it's seen in everyday life depicted on the 50-yen coin.

Ukiyo-e

Ukiyo-e is a genre of Japanese art which flourished from the 17th through 19th centuries.
Its artists produced woodblock prints and paintings of such subjects as female beauties; kabuki actors and sumo wrestlers; scenes from history and folk tales; travel scenes and landscapes; flora and fauna; and erotica.
